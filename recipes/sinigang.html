Sinigang

Sinigang is a sour soup native to the Philippines. This recipe uses pork as the main ingredient.

Other proteins and seafood can also be used. Beef, shrimp, fish are commonly used to cook sinigang.

The chicken version, on the other hand, is called sinampalukang manok.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly or buto-buto
  • 1 bunch of spinach or kang-kong
  • 3 tablespoons of fish sauce (patis)
  • 12 pieces of string beans sitaw, cut in 2 inch length
  • 2 pieces tomato quartered
  • 3 pieces chili or banana pepper
  • 1 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 quarts water
  • 1 piece onion sliced
  • 2 pieces taro gabi, quartered
  • 1 piece sinigang mix good for 2 liters of water

Steps on how to cook Adobo

  1. Heat the pot and put in the cooking oil
  2. Saute the onion until its layers separate from each other
  3. Add the pork belly and cook until its outer part turns light brown
  4. Put in the fish sauce and mix the ingredients
  5. Pour the water and bring it to boil
  6. Add the taro and tomatoes then simmer for 40 minutes or until pork is tender
  7. Put in the sinigang mix and chili
  8. Add the string beans (and other vegetables if there are any) and simmer for 5 to 8 minutes
  9. Put in the spinach, turn off the heat, and cover the pot. Let the spinach cook using the remaining heat in the pot
  10. Serve hot. Share and enjoy
